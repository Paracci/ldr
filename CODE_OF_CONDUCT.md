# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

LDR is an open standard built by and for the community.
We pledge to make participation in this project a welcoming experience
for everyone, regardless of background, experience level, or affiliation.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Be respectful and constructive in all interactions
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Give and receive feedback graciously
- Focus on what is best for the standard and the community
- Acknowledge differing viewpoints and experiences

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Personal attacks, insults, or harassment of any kind
- Dismissing contributions based on who made them, not what they contain
- Attempting to steer the standard toward a single company's commercial interests
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable
behavior and will take fair corrective action in response to any behavior
that violates this code.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions that
do not align with this code of conduct, and to temporarily or permanently
ban contributors for behavior they deem inappropriate.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, PRs,
discussions, and any other forum where community members interact on behalf
of this project.

## A Note on Commercial Interests

LDR is a community-owned open standard. No company — including any that
contributed significantly to its development — has special authority over
the direction of the standard.

Contributions from companies and their employees are welcome and valued.
However, attempts to gain undue control over the standard, registry
infrastructure, or governance process are against the spirit of this project
and will be addressed by the community.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private
issue or contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be reviewed
promptly and handled with discretion.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this code of conduct may be
removed from their role by decision of the broader community.
